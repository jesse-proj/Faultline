The Issue

According to the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), poverty is not a single experience. It exists in four distinct types.

Absolute & Relative

Absolute Poverty: Unable to meet a minimum standard of living (approx. 3% of Singaporeans).

Relative Poverty: Basic needs are met, but deprived of typical societal opportunities (approx. 25% of Singaporeans).

Situational & Generational

Situational: Caused by sudden changes (medical emergency, job loss, natural disaster). Usually temporary with government aid.

Intergenerational: Chronic poverty passed down. Extremely difficult to escape due to systemic resource lack.

Deprivation Causes
Inadequate Housing Intense climate change events frequently wipe out vulnerable settlements, leaving poor communities trapped in substandard housing without the resources to rebuild safely.
Bad Health Labor exploitation and aggressive raw resource extraction severely pollute local environments and expose workers to physical hazards without adequate protective gear or healthcare.
Lacking Education Globalized industries extract wealth and rely on cheap manual labor, forcing children into work instead of school while local education systems remain chronically underfunded.

What we Believe

Poverty is not a single experience — it is absolute deprivation, generational entrapment, and systemic exclusion all at once. Millions are denied adequate housing, nutrition, healthcare, and education not by choice, but by circumstance. We believe that no one should starve because they cannot afford food, and no one should have to sacrifice their wellbeing just to barely survive. Social stigma and inequality only deepen these cycles, making escape nearly impossible without collective action. We stand for a world where every person's basic needs are met — and where poverty is treated as the solvable injustice it truly is.

Call to Action

How ordinary citizens can actively help break the cycles of global poverty and exploitation.

🛍️

Support Local Industry

Break the trap of raw resource extraction by buying locally manufactured goods. This everyday choice strengthens domestic industries, generates local jobs, and reduces national dependence on low-value raw material exports.

🤝

Ethical Consumption

Fight labor exploitation by choosing goods from companies that prioritize fair wages and safe working conditions. Demand supply chain transparency and support collective bargaining rights to ensure worker dignity.

🌱

Community Climate Action

Protect vulnerable communities from climate-induced poverty by participating in local environmental sustainability efforts. Grassroots resilience—like creating green spaces and advocating clean energy—defends against devastating climate risks.
