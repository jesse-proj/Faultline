The Weight of Inequality

The effects of colonialism continue to echo today. The economic disparity between the Global North and South is driven by historical resource extraction and labor exploitation.

The Wealth Drain

The richest 1% owns nearly half the world's wealth. The current system extracts wealth from the Global South to the Global North at an astonishing rate of $30 million an hour.

  • In 2024, billionaire wealth increased by $2 trillion.
  • An average of four new billionaires are created per week.
  • 60% of billionaire wealth is unearned (inheritance, cronyism).

The Colonial Root

The World Bank notes that 26 of the world's poorest countries are primarily in Africa. Experts cite colonialism as a core factor stunting growth.

  • Historical losses of human capital stunt modern GDP.
  • Poverty in Africa is multi-dimensional (climate risks, colonial legacies, poor health systems).
417 Million

Children in low- and middle-income countries are severely deprived in at least two vital areas critical for their health and development. 65% of children in low-income countries lack access to a basic toilet.

Deconstructing Poverty

According to the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), poverty is not a single experience. It exists in four distinct types.

Absolute & Relative

Absolute Poverty: Unable to meet a minimum standard of living (approx. 3% of Singaporeans).

Relative Poverty: Basic needs are met, but deprived of typical societal opportunities (approx. 25% of Singaporeans).

Situational & Generational

Situational: Caused by sudden changes (medical emergency, job loss, natural disaster). Usually temporary with government aid.

Intergenerational: Chronic poverty passed down. Extremely difficult to escape due to systemic resource lack.

Multi-dimensional Deprivation Impact & Manifestation
Adequate Housing Absence of safe, habitable shelter. Key issues: affordability and security.
Good Health Driven by food insecurity (lack of nutrition, not just hunger) and inadequate mental healthcare.
Adequate Education Systemic inequity where lower-income families cannot access quality primary education.
Social Exclusion Stigma and isolation preventing upward social and economic mobility.

Paths to Progress

Despite the harsh realities, targeted government interventions, economic growth, and global philanthropy are actively lifting millions out of poverty.

Success in the Philippines

Self-rated poverty dropped from 54% to 37% by the end of 2025. This means 4.5 million families no longer see themselves as poor.

  • Food poverty declined from 49% to 19%.
  • Driven by 5.5% economic growth.
  • Supported by cash transfers and targeted fuel subsidies for drivers and farmers.

Global Philanthropy

The United States leads in financial philanthropy, contributing $49 billion annually (0.23% of GNI).

However, when factoring in volunteering and helping strangers, the 2023 World Giving Index highlights Indonesia, Ukraine, Kenya, and Liberia as top-ranking nations.
